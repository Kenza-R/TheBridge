BRIDGE — Healthcare Worker Mental Health Companion
Frontend Spec for Lovable
==========================================================

Build this in React (or React Native / Expo if mobile). The tone rules below are
non-negotiable — especially no red, no clinical language, no alarm hierarchy.


==========================================================
SECTION 1 — PRODUCT OVERVIEW & CORE PHILOSOPHY
==========================================================

ONE-SENTENCE DEFINITION:
Bridge detects physician burnout before it becomes crisis — using EHR workflow
signals and wearable biometrics — then routes clinicians to peer support through a
privacy-first architecture that is entirely controlled by the physician, never the
employer.

WHAT BRIDGE IS:
- An ambient signal detector for distress
- A physician-controlled peer support launcher
- A safe, low-friction decompression space
- A privacy-first trust architecture

WHAT BRIDGE IS NOT:
- Not a surveillance tool for HR or administration
- Not a crisis hotline
- Not a mandatory wellness check-in
- Not something that shares individual data with employers

CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
- Physician sovereignty — every control belongs to them
- No auto-send — humans approve every outreach
- Employer firewall — hospital never sees individual data
- Friction minimalism — max 2 taps to any feature
- Non-clinical tone — language that does not pathologize

SEVEN-STEP PRODUCT FLOW (build UI for all 7):
1. Signal Detection — app silently scores EHR + wearable + self-report data
2. Threshold Trigger — when distress score crosses threshold, soft in-app banner appears
3. Three-Option Interface — Reset Now / Talk to Someone / Handle Admin (equal weight)
4. Physician Decision — physician chooses their path, no auto-escalation ever
5. Circle of Trust Approval Gate — physician selects who to notify, previews message, sends
6. Peer Response — circle member receives private in-app message, can respond or schedule
7. Recovery Loop — 48h later, one optional "how are you feeling?" question


==========================================================
SECTION 3 — CIRCLE OF TRUST (drives 3 screens)
==========================================================

WHAT IT IS:
A private list of 2-6 trusted colleagues each physician builds during onboarding.
No one outside the physician's selected circle can see its membership.
The hospital cannot see who is in whose circle.

ONBOARDING FLOW (5 steps):
1. Search colleagues by name or specialty (Bridge users from same hospital only)
2. Colleague receives in-app invite, can accept / decline / accept-with-limits
3. Assign roles: First Reach / Backup / Check-In Buddy
4. Set notification preferences per member: Always notify / Notify after 4h / Manual only
5. Set threshold: "Always ask me" / "Suggest after 2 hard days" / "Never suggest"

LIVE CIRCLE LAUNCH FLOW (when physician taps "Talk to Someone"):
- Circle members appear as cards: name, role, specialty, availability status
- Availability status pulled from Epic (in surgery = unavailable, off shift = green)
- Physician selects 1+ members
- Pre-filled default message: "I had a rough one. Could use a quick chat when you're free." — fully editable
- FULL-SCREEN MESSAGE PREVIEW before send — physician sees exactly what recipient sees
- Single "Send" tap dispatches the message
- Confirmation: "Your message is on its way."

ANTI-SURVEILLANCE RULE:
Hospital admin dashboard shows only: "X% of physicians used peer support this month."
No names. No who-contacted-whom. No message content. Not even department breakdown
below a group size of 10.

THIRD-PARTY CONCERN FLAGGING ("Someone on My Radar"):
- Physician goes to Team tab → selects a colleague → "I'm a bit worried about them"
- Optional private note (never visible to employer)
- The concerned physician is never identified to the flagged colleague
- Flagged colleague sees: "A colleague is thinking of you" — no name of who flagged them
- Flags auto-expire after 14 days

SECTION 6 — NOTIFICATIONS (use this copy verbatim)
==========================================================

DESIGN RULES:
- No clinical language. No alarm words ("crisis", "emergency", "alert").
- No red badges. No sound during patient care hours (default silent 7am-7pm).
- Every notification has a single clear action.
- Maximum 1 notification per day per physician.
- The notification is an invitation, not a directive.

TRIGGER NOTIFICATIONS (what the physician receives):

TRIGGER: Composite score high for 3 consecutive days
  Title: "Hey — today's a good day to check in"
  Body: "You've had a stretch of heavy shifts. Bridge has a few things that might help."
  Action: [Open Bridge]

TRIGGER: Patient death documented (physician was primary)
  Title: "That was a hard moment"
  Body: "Losing a patient is one of the hardest things we face. Your circle is here if you want to talk."
  Actions: [Open Bridge] [See my circle]

TRIGGER: HRV + sleep both low for 4+ days
  Title: "Your body is telling you something"
  Body: "Your HRV has been lower than usual, and your sleep has been choppy. A 2-minute reset might help."
  Action: [Try a breathing reset]

TRIGGER: 7 days since last peer connection (if score elevated)
  Title: "It's been a while since you talked to anyone"
  Body: "Dr. Chen is available this afternoon. Sometimes 10 minutes with the right person makes a week better."
  Actions: [Reach out] [Maybe later]

TRIGGER: Colleague flagged this physician
  Title: "A colleague is thinking of you"
  Body: "Someone in your network wanted to check in. No obligation — just a reminder that you're not alone."
  Actions: [See my circle] [I'm good, thanks]

TRIGGER: Score drops significantly (recovery)
  Title: "You seem to be doing better"
  Body: "The last few days look lighter than last week. Keep it up."
  Action: [See my trend]

CIRCLE MEMBER NOTIFICATIONS (what peers receive):

When physician initiates outreach:
  Title: "Dr. Patel reached out"
  Body: [the physician's message]
  Actions: [Respond] [Schedule a call]

When physician had hard trigger but did NOT initiate (only if physician opted in):
  Title: "Check in with Dr. Patel when you can"
  Body: "They've had a hard few days. No pressure — even a quick 'hey' goes a long way."
  Action: [Send a note]


==========================================================
SECTION 9 — FRONTEND SCREENS (build all 10)
==========================================================

TECH STACK:
React Native (Expo SDK 51+) with TypeScript
Navigation: Expo Router (file-based)
State: Zustand + React Query
Animations: Reanimated 3 + Moti
No generic UI library — build custom primitives
Fonts: SF Pro Display (iOS) / Inter (Android)
Colors: navy palette (#04091a, #0c1a2e) with electric teal (#06b6d4) accent

------------------------------
S01 — ONBOARDING: WELCOME
Route: /onboarding/welcome
------------------------------
Purpose: First impression. Establish trust and tone. Not clinical. Not alarming.

Components:
- Background: Full-screen animated dark navy gradient. Subtle particle field
  (slow drift, 12px white dots at 4% opacity). NOT a hospital setting image.
- Logo: "Bridge" wordmark, center, white. Syne 900 font. Tagline below:
  "Built for physicians, by physicians." DM Sans 400.
- CTA: Single rounded button "Get Started" — electric teal background.
  Below: "Already have an account? Sign in" in muted text.

Animations:
- Logo fades in over 800ms
- Tagline slides up with 300ms delay
- CTA fades in last at 1200ms
- Haptic feedback on CTA tap

------------------------------
S02 — ONBOARDING: HOSPITAL VERIFICATION
Route: /onboarding/verify
------------------------------
Components:
- Header: "Connect to your hospital"
- SSO Button: "Sign in with [Hospital Name] SSO" — primary path
- Alternative: "Use work email" input + NPI number input
- Security note (small, tappable to expand): "Bridge never sees your EHR patient
  data. We only request read access to workflow metadata like login times and note counts."

Error States:
- SSO failure: "Couldn't connect to your hospital's system. Try your work email instead."
- NPI not found: "We couldn't verify that NPI. Double-check the number or contact support."

------------------------------
S03 — ONBOARDING: BUILD YOUR CIRCLE
Route: /onboarding/circle
------------------------------
Purpose: CRITICAL screen. If physician builds a circle, they are 3x more likely to
use Bridge during a hard moment. Make this feel personal and safe, not bureaucratic.

Components:
- Header copy: "Who do you trust when things get hard?"
  NOT "Select peer contacts." NOT "Configure notification recipients."
- Search bar: search colleagues by name. Shows Bridge users from same hospital only.
  Cards: photo, name, specialty, "Accepted invitation" badge.
- Circle visualization: As physicians are added, they appear as avatar circles
  arranged around a central "You" node. Max 6 slots. Animated — new additions
  pulse into position.
- Role assignment (inline): Tap a circle member → bottom sheet →
  "First Reach / Backup / Check-In Buddy" toggle.
- Skip option: "Set this up later" in small text. No guilt-trip.

UX Notes:
- Empty state: placeholder avatar circles pulse softly with a "+" icon
- After adding 2+ members: warm confirmation card slides in at bottom:
  "Your circle is taking shape. They'll be here when you need them."
- Invites sent in real-time

------------------------------
S04 — ONBOARDING: WEARABLE CONNECT
Route: /onboarding/wearables
------------------------------
Components:
- Header: "Optional: connect your wearable" with small "Why?" tooltip
- Device tiles: Apple Watch, Garmin, WHOOP, Oura, Fitbit — each as a card
- Privacy promise (prominent): "Your biometric numbers never leave your device.
  We only use anonymized trend patterns."
- Skip: "Skip for now — add later in Settings."

------------------------------
S05 — HOME SCREEN
Route: /home
------------------------------
Purpose: Default screen. Calm, not data-heavy. Show only what matters right now.

Components (top to bottom):
- Top bar: Hospital logo (left) + notification bell (right, subtle blue dot if unread).
  NO score displayed on home — reduces anxiety about "checking your score."
- Time-aware greeting: "Good morning, Dr. Patel" / "Good evening"
  Below: rotating daily insight card. Examples:
  "Your HRV has been stable this week."
  "You've used peer support 3x this month."
  "You're on a 5-day breathing streak."
- Quick Actions row: Three icon buttons — Breathe / My Circle / Journal
  60px diameter circles, subtle navy background.
- Today card: If trigger is active → show three-option interface (see S06).
  If no trigger → show "Daily Check-In" prompt (one-question mood tap, 5 emoji, optional).
- Recent Activity feed: Last 3 events as tappable cards.
  "You did a breathing session yesterday"
  "Dr. Chen responded to your message"
  "Your sleep was better than average last night"
- Bottom tab bar: Home / Circle / Wellness / Team / Settings
  5 tabs. Current tab: electric teal underline.

------------------------------
S06 — THREE-OPTION INTERFACE (trigger state)
Route: /home (trigger state)
------------------------------
Purpose: Most critical interaction in the product. Must feel like a kind check-in
from a colleague, not a medical alert.

Header copy (pull from copy library of ~20 variations):
- "You've had a tough stretch."
- "This was a hard shift."
- "That was a lot to carry."
NEVER: "Your distress score is high."
NEVER: "You may be experiencing burnout."

Three Option Cards (all same size, equal visual weight):

Card 1 — Reset Now (teal border)
  Icon: leaf or wave (NOT a heart or cross)
  Title: "Take a breath."
  Subtitle: "90-second reset, right here."
  → taps into S07 (Breathing)

Card 2 — Talk to Someone (blue border)
  Icon: two abstract shapes (NOT a speech bubble cliché)
  Title: "Reach out."
  Subtitle: "Your circle is here."
  → taps into S08 (Circle launch)

Card 3 — Handle Admin (muted border)
  Icon: calendar dot
  Title: "I've got this."
  Subtitle: "Dismiss for now or schedule a check-in."
  → confirms dismiss + optionally schedules 1-day or 3-day snooze

Layout Rules:
- All three cards are the same size — equal visual weight. No card is "more important."
- NO RED anywhere on this screen. Use teal, blue, and muted navy only.
- Cards animate in with staggered slide-up (100ms delay between each).
- Swipe-to-dismiss is NOT allowed — physician must tap a card or the X to dismiss.

------------------------------
S07 — BREATHING / MICRO-RESET
Route: /wellness/breathe
------------------------------
Components:
- Full-screen animation: A soft orb (300px diameter on iPhone 14) that expands
  on inhale, contracts on exhale. Color: deep teal-to-navy gradient during inhale,
  inverts during exhale. Smooth cubic-bezier easing (not linear).
- Instruction text: Large, centered. "Breathe in" / "Hold" / "Breathe out"
  Syne font, white, 28px. Fades between states with 200ms crossfade.
- Pattern selector (bottom sheet, pre-session):
  Box Breathing (4-4-4-4, ~64s) / 4-7-8 (~57s) / Extended Exhale (~72s)
  Default: Box Breathing.
- Progress ring: Thin arc around the orb that fills over session duration.
- End screen: "Nice work." (not "Session complete")
  Optional: "How do you feel?" 1-5 emoji scale.
  Optional: "Share this moment with your circle" (sends: "I just took a breath. Feeling a bit better.")
  Skip is the default.

------------------------------
S08 — CIRCLE OF TRUST LAUNCH
Route: /circle/launch
------------------------------
Components:
- Header: "Who do you want to reach?"
- Member cards: horizontal scrollable list. Avatar + name + availability status badge.
  Green dot = off shift. Gray dot = on shift. Red X = do not disturb (self-set).
  Never shows exact location.
- Pre-filled message field: "I had a rough one. Could use a quick chat when you're free."
  Fully editable. 280 character limit.
- Full-screen message preview before send.
  Prominent "Cancel" and "Send" buttons — equal visual weight, "Send" is teal.
- Additional options at bottom of preview:
  "Request a walk together" / "Schedule 15 minutes to talk"
- Confirmation after send: "Your message is on its way. Dr. Chen typically responds
  within 2 hours." (based on historical pattern, not a promise)

------------------------------
S09 — WELLNESS TAB (Meditation Library)
Route: /wellness
------------------------------
Components:
- Tabs within Wellness: Breathing / Meditations / Debrief / My Trends
- Meditation cards: Title, duration, description (12 words max).
  Cover art: abstract dark imagery (no stock hospital photos).
  Tags: "Post-shift" / "Night shift" / "After loss" / "High-volume day"
- Tracks (10 total, all under 5 min):
  "After the code" (4 min)
  "End of shift exhale" (3 min)
  "Between patients" (2 min)
  "Night shift grounding" (5 min)
  "Imposter moment" (4 min)
  + 5 more
- Audio player: full-screen dark mode. Waveform animation (not a progress bar).
  Large artwork. Back 10s / Play / Forward 10s controls.
- Offline mode: all tracks pre-downloaded on first WiFi connection.

------------------------------
S10 — MY TRENDS (Private)
Route: /wellness/trends
------------------------------
Components:
- Wellbeing chart: 90-day smooth line chart.
  Y-axis labeled "Wellbeing" (not "Distress Score"). Higher = better. Teal color.
  Events overlaid as dots (tap to expand: "Lost a patient" / "Peer support session")
- Insight cards (AI-generated, plain English, max 3):
  "Your score dips on weeks with more than 2 night shifts."
  "Peer support sessions correlate with faster score recovery for you."
- Data controls: "What does this include?" expander — shows data sources
  (EHR signals, wearable, self-report) with toggles to remove each source.
  Physician can opt out of any layer at any time.
