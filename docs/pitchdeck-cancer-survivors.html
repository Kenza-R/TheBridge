03 / 11
The Adherence Crisis

Half of patients stop
their medication. Nobody notices.

  • 50% discontinue endocrine therapy within 5 years. For breast cancer survivors, this is the difference between remission and recurrence. Side effects and psychological distress are the primary drivers.
  • 60% of survivors never receive a Survivorship Care Plan. Commission on Cancer (CoC) mandates it. 1,600 accredited programs are out of compliance. No scalable tool exists.
  • 2–3× higher ED utilization among survivors with unmanaged distress — $3,000–$8,000/survivor/year in excess acute care that payers are paying today.
  • Zero condition-specific mental health infrastructure. Generic platforms (Lyra, Spring) have no oncology specificity. EHRs have no survivorship engagement layer. No peer network exists at scale.
04 / 11
The Solution

A living platform for the
cancer journey after treatment.

01
Living Survivorship Care Plan (SCP) Solves CoC compliance instantly. Dynamic document updated at each follow-up. Contains treatment history, symptom monitoring, adherence tracking, and escalation routes — all accessible to survivor and oncology team.
02
Peer Network — Cancer-Type Matched Breast cancer survivors connect with someone 18 months ahead of them. Fear of recurrence, scanxiety, chemo brain, sexual health — matched by cancer type, treatment, and stage. No generic support groups.
03
Adherence & Distress Monitoring Validated PRO collection (PROMIS, COST tool). Endocrine therapy adherence tracking. Event-triggered outreach on scan days. Escalation pathways to oncology team and licensed therapists.
