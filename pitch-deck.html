The Asset

The youngest cells that exist.
Available once.

Stem cells are liquid gold. The placenta is the richest deposit on earth.
Neonatal placental MSCs have the highest potency of any stem cells the body can yield. Potency, colony-forming efficiency, and immunosuppressive capacity decline sharply with age.
Cells preserved at birth retain their zero-day epigenetic age indefinitely. A 60-year-old using these cells accesses the biology of a newborn.
The placenta is immunologically privileged by design, yielding cells that resist rejection across populations.
Amniotic epithelial cells — one of six compartments banked

Business Model

Two revenue streams.
One compounding asset.

01 — Family Banking
Premium enrollment + annual cryostorage. Multi-decade recurring contracts from families engaged with fertility treatment, longevity protocols, and premium obstetric care.

Benchmark: CBR charges $3,500 enroll + $200/yr for cord blood alone (one compartment, infant only). Cell4Life and Anja Health reach $4,500–$6,000 for cord blood + tissue bundles. TFR targets $9,500–$12,000 enrollment + $500–$700/yr for six compartments, both donors, geo-distributed.
02 — Institutional Supply
Characterized, viability-tested MSC populations sold to clinical researchers, hospital cell therapy programs, and allogeneic therapy developers.

Supply agreements with CDMOs and anonymous donation programs offset collection costs at scale.

The repository is the moat. A growing collection of characterized, long-term stored material takes years to build and cannot be replicated.
