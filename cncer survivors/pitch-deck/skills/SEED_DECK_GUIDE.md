# Seed pitch deck guide (YC + top-tier VC screening)

Structure tuned for **Y Combinator** application review and **seed partner** first meetings. Each slide answers one question investors ask in the first 3 minutes.

## Standard flow (11 slides)

| # | Slide | YC screen | Partner screen |
|---|--------|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | **Title** | What do you do? (one line) | Identity — no fluff |
| 2 | **Problem** | Is pain real & urgent? | Specific pain + data (+ why now inside) |
| 3 | **Solution** | 10× better than status quo? | Direct fix to problem stated |
| 4 | **Product** | What does it look like? | Demo / workflow, not feature list |
| 5 | **Traction** | Any proof? | Signal > vanity; plan OK at pre-seed |
| 6 | **Market** | Big enough? | Bottom-up TAM / SAM / SOM + sources |
| 7 | **Business model** | How do you make money? | Unit economics thinking |
| 8 | **Go-to-market** | How do you get customers? | Specific wedge, not “viral” |
| 9 | **Competition** | Why you? | Honest map + moat |
| 10 | **Team** | Can you execute? | Founder–market fit |
| 11 | **The ask** | What do you need? | Amount, use of funds, milestones |

## Screening killers (avoid)

- Top-down “1% of $500B healthcare” with no math  
- No traction slide (even pre-seed needs LOIs, pilots, or crisp milestones)  
- “No competitors”  
- Consumer-only monetization for B2B health infrastructure  
- 20+ slides or walls of text  
- Vague ask (“raising a round”)  

## Screening winners

- **One idea per slide**, large type, cited stats  
- **Traction early** (slide 5) — YC reads this before market  
- **Distribution** spelled out (slide 8) — “how you get users”  
- **Bottom-up market** with wedge (breast HR+ → expand)  
- **Team** tied to this exact problem (oncology + VBC + regulated SaaS)  

## 3-minute verbal pitch order

Same as slides: Problem → Solution → Traction → Market → Model → GTM → Team → Ask.
